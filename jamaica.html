Reggae Hostel

I spent a day in Kingston to go record collecting. I stayed in Reggae Hostel in Half-Way Tree in uptown Kingston

Reggae Hostel

Rasta graffiti all over the walls. All the rooms were named after different genres of Jamaican music (ska, mento, dancehall etc.)

Derrick Harriot's Record Shop

My hostel was around the corner from a record shop owned by Derrick Harriott, the Jamaican singer and producer known for such classics as "The Loser" and "Do I Wory". I went twice and it was closed both times, but it was still cool to see it.

Me and Lucas

Me and my friend Lucas, a Swiss ex-patriot and rastafarian who has lived in Kingston since the 60's. He is a record collector and the biggest ebay seller of Jamaican records on the island. One of the coolest people I've ever met. Oh and he sold me the first records I've ever owned.

My first records

What I'm holding here in my hands is the very first recording of Guns Fever by Baba Brooks, a ska classic. Almost a year later and I still can't believe that this is something I have.

My first LP

My first LP, the Birth of Ska. There are not many places in Jamaica where you can get vintage records like this anymore, but they're still around if you know where to go and who to talk to.

Vinyl Thursday Food Truck

I went to a vinyl night in Kingston called Vinyl Thursdays, that night they were playing roots reggae and dub. There was a food truck outside serving vegan food, and I got to try ackee and callaloo for the first time.
